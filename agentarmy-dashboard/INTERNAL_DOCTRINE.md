## 1. Purpose

This document defines the principled boundaries of two foundational subsystems within AgentArmy OS:

1. **Defensive Intelligence Substructure (DIS)** — the private strategic intelligence organ
2. **QubitCoin (QC)** — the dormant crypto-economic engine

These constraints exist to protect the platform, its users, and its long-term viability. They serve as the anchor for any future licensing, sale, or exclusivity negotiation — a clear, principled core that exists independently of the code.

---

## 2. What DIS Is Allowed To Do

### 2.1 Permitted Operations

- **Monitor the AI ecosystem** — scan open-source releases, commercial model announcements, adversarial technique disclosures, regulatory changes, competitor launches, and research publications.
- **Classify signals** — categorize detected signals as threats, opportunities, or neutral using confidence-scored classification.
- **Run simulations** — simulate the impact of threats and opportunities on AgentArmy's strategic position, producing net scores and compound risk assessments.
- **Generate upgrade proposals** — produce structured proposals (new models, defenses, heuristics, agents, tools, safety rules, countermeasures, optimizations, regulatory adaptations) with priority, risk, effort, and expected-gain scoring.
- **Queue proposals for review** — all proposals enter a pending state and wait for explicit root-owner approval. DIS never self-approves.
- **Feed ML adaptation** — provide simulation data to the Machine Learning Layer for continuous threat-landscape modeling.
- **Anticipate threats** — extrapolate top-risk signals forward using predictive analytics and strategic foresight models.
- **Generate intelligence reports** — produce full reports containing signals, threats, opportunities, simulations, proposals, risk levels, version lineage, and strategic notes.
- **Emit events** — broadcast scan, simulation, proposal, approval, rejection, rollout, and alert events for integration with Civilization Intelligence and the TSU event bus.

### 2.2 Governance Constraint

Every proposal generated by DIS must be explicitly reviewed by the root-owner before any action is taken. The system supports three review outcomes:

| Action   | Effect                                       |
|----------|----------------------------------------------|
| Approve  | Moves to approved queue; eligible for rollout |
| Reject   | Permanently rejected; logged                  |
| Defer    | Shelved for later review; remains in backlog  |

Only approved proposals may be included in a version rollout.

---

## 3. What DIS Is Never Allowed To Do

- **Self-approve proposals.** DIS generates; the root-owner decides. No autonomous code integration.
- **Expose intelligence to tenants.** DIS data, reports, and proposals are never visible to any user, API, or public dashboard.
- **Communicate externally.** DIS does not send data outside the OS. No web requests, API calls, or telemetry to external services.
- **Modify running subsystems.** DIS proposes changes; it does not apply them. Rollout is a separate, root-owner-gated process.
- **Override safety kernel decisions.** The Integrity Safety Kernel and Constitutional Grid remain authoritative. DIS cannot bypass or weaken safety constraints.
- **Access user data.** DIS operates on ecosystem signals, not user content. It never reads, processes, or stores tenant or end-user data.
- **Operate when frozen.** If the root-owner freezes a subsystem or the full OS, DIS halts immediately.

## 4. What QubitCoin Is Allowed To Do

### 4.1 Permitted Operations (When Active)

- **Issue coins on usage** — reward users with QC for qualifying platform actions, following the halving schedule.
- **Deposit to treasury** — route the platform's share (75%) of all issuance to the treasury wallet.
- **Enable transfers** — allow user-to-user QC transfers with a fee (0.1%) routed to treasury.
- **Enable trades** — allow marketplace trades between wallets, tagged for analytics.
- **Burn supply** — permanently destroy QC from any wallet to increase scarcity.
- **Execute buybacks** — treasury purchases QC from the market to support price floor.
- **Award milestones and referrals** — distribute bonus QC for platform milestones and user referrals.
- **Accept spend** — allow users to spend QC on premium compute and tool unlocks, with funds routed to treasury.
- **Report metrics** — expose supply, issuance curve, scarcity index, treasury snapshot, and halving schedule via the War Room console.
- **Emit events** — broadcast activation, issuance, transfer, trade, burn, buyback, treasury-invest, halving, freeze, and milestone events.

### 4.2 Treasury Investment

The root-owner may direct treasury funds into the following categories:

| Category     | Purpose                                    |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Compute      | Premium compute infrastructure              |
| Research     | R&D for platform capabilities               |
| Partnerships | Strategic alliances and integrations        |
| Marketing    | Growth and adoption campaigns               |
| Security     | Defensive measures and audits               |
| Reserves     | Rainy-day fund for stability                |

Every investment requires the root-owner's private activation key.

## 5. What QubitCoin Is Never Allowed To Do

- **Activate without root-owner key.** The engine starts dormant. Only the root-owner, using a key of at least 8 characters, can bring it online.
- **Self-activate.** No subsystem, timer, or external event can trigger activation. It is fully manual.
- **Mint beyond the cap.** Total supply is hard-capped at 21,000,000,000 QC. The engine refuses issuance beyond this.
- **Skip halving.** The 5-epoch, 10-year halving schedule is immutable. Once activated, epoch progression is time-based and cannot be accelerated, paused, or reverted.
- **Transfer from frozen wallets.** Frozen wallets cannot send or receive.
- **Bypass fee collection.** Every transfer incurs a 0.1% fee routed to treasury. There are no fee exemptions.
- **Expose treasury controls publicly.** Treasury investment, buyback, and burn operations are root-owner-only. No public API, no tenant access.
- **Operate when engine is frozen.** If the root-owner freezes the engine, all issuance, transfers, trades, burns, and buybacks halt immediately.

## 6. Conditions for QubitCoin Activation

QubitCoin may be activated **only** when ALL of the following conditions are met:

1. **Root-owner decision.** The root-owner explicitly decides to activate. This is a business decision, not a technical trigger.
2. **Platform stability.** The OS must be running with all critical subsystems healthy (as reported by TSU).
3. **Sufficient user base.** Activation should occur when the platform has enough active users that the token has genuine utility and demand.
4. **Legal clearance.** The root-owner has assessed applicable securities, tax, and crypto regulations in target jurisdictions.
5. **Private key ceremony.** The root-owner provides a private key (min 8 characters) through the War Room console. This key is hashed and stored; it is required for all subsequent privileged operations (freeze, unfreeze, treasury investment).
6. **No active critical threats.** DIS should report a global risk level of 'calm' or 'watch'. Activation during 'elevated' or 'critical' risk is discouraged.

### 6.1 Post-Activation

Once activated:
- Genesis allocation (1% of max supply) is minted to the treasury.
- The halving clock starts.
- All usage-based rewards begin flowing.
- The engine cannot be returned to 'dormant'. It can only be frozen/unfrozen.

## 7. Licensing and Transfer Provisions

This doctrine and the subsystems it governs are designed for clean transfer:

- **Version packaging:** The Upgrade Governance panel can package any version for licensing or sale, including full proposal lineage and changelog.
- **Compliance export:** The Master Control panel can export a compliance bundle covering all subsystem statuses, version history, DIS reports, and QC ledger.
- **Ownership transfer:** The Master Control panel includes a root-owner transfer protocol. Upon transfer, the new root-owner must provide their own activation key.
- **Doctrine transfer:** This document transfers with the platform. The new owner inherits all constraints and may modify them only as root-owner.
